# Debate Resolutions — Liberty, Money, Government & Economics

Organized by theme. Each is designed to produce a strong debate with well-sourced evidence on both sides. Starred (⭐) resolutions are my picks for the most compelling debates with the deepest evidence bases.

---

## Central Banking & The Federal Reserve

**⭐ "Should the United States abolish the Federal Reserve?"**
- PRO: Austrian business cycle theory, moral hazard, Cantillon effects, wealth inequality driven by money printing, track record of bubbles (dot-com, housing, everything bubble), loss of 97% of dollar purchasing power since 1913
- CON: Lender of last resort prevents bank runs, monetary policy smooths recessions, deflation spirals without elastic currency, pre-Fed panic of 1907/1893 era instability

**"Has Federal Reserve monetary policy since 2008 done more harm than good?"**
- PRO: Asset inflation, zombie companies, wealth gap explosion, moral hazard (too big to fail), 2021-2023 inflation as direct consequence of QE
- CON: Prevented Great Depression 2.0, saved the financial system, COVID response prevented economic collapse, inflation was transitory(ish) and came down

**"Should the Federal Reserve be audited and its operations made fully transparent to the public?"**
- PRO: $4.7 trillion balance sheet managed with minimal oversight, revolving door with Wall Street, public has a right to know how money supply decisions are made
- CON: Political interference in monetary policy would be worse, markets need Fed credibility, operational independence produces better outcomes

## Hard Money, Gold & Bitcoin

**⭐ "Should the United States return to a gold standard or adopt a hard money policy?"**
- PRO: Prevents government from inflating away savings, historically stable prices 1800-1913, disciplines fiscal policy, gold as Schelling point, eliminates Cantillon effect
- CON: Deflationary spirals, gold supply doesn't match economic growth, Great Depression worsened by gold standard rigidity, not enough gold to back modern economy

**⭐ "Is Bitcoin a superior monetary system to fiat currency?"**
- PRO: Fixed supply (21M cap), censorship resistant, permissionless, no central authority, provably scarce, separation of money and state, El Salvador experiment, store of value thesis
- CON: Volatility makes it unsuitable as unit of account, energy consumption, scalability limitations, deflationary currency discourages spending, criminal use, no lender of last resort

**"Is inflation a hidden tax on the middle class and poor?"**
- PRO: Asset holders (wealthy) benefit from inflation while wage earners and savers lose purchasing power, Cantillon effects distribute new money to those closest to the spigot first
- CON: Moderate inflation reduces real debt burden (helps borrowers including homeowners), deflation is worse for workers, wages eventually adjust

## Austrian Economics vs. Keynesian/MMT

**⭐ "Is Keynesian economics or Austrian economics a better framework for managing a modern economy?"**
- PRO Austrian: Malinvestment theory explains boom-bust cycles, Hayek's knowledge problem proves central planning fails, Say's Law, price signals as information, Mises' calculation problem
- PRO Keynesian: Aggregate demand management works empirically, liquidity traps are real, fiscal multipliers documented, paradox of thrift, 2008-2009 stimulus evidence

**"Is Modern Monetary Theory (MMT) a viable framework for fiscal policy?"**
- PRO MMT: Sovereign currency issuers can't go bankrupt in own currency, inflation is the real constraint not deficit, functional finance, Japan as proof (200%+ debt/GDP without crisis)
- CON: Inflation is a real constraint that's hard to control politically, Weimar/Zimbabwe/Venezuela, no mechanism to cut spending once started, misunderstands money creation

**"Does government stimulus spending create genuine economic growth or merely misallocate resources?"**
- PRO stimulus: Keynesian multiplier, 2009 ARRA prevented deeper recession, infrastructure creates real value, automatic stabilizers
- CON: Crowding out private investment, Bastiat's broken window fallacy, seen vs unseen, malinvestment, stimulus becomes permanent spending

## Size & Role of Government

**⭐ "Would the United States be better off with a dramatically smaller federal government?"**
- PRO: Regulatory burden ($1.9T annual compliance costs per CEI), public choice theory (concentrated benefits/diffuse costs), Laffer curve, Hong Kong/Singapore models, 10th Amendment federalism
- CON: Public goods, externalities, social safety net prevents suffering, economies of scale in defense/infrastructure, historical evidence from Gilded Age inequality

**"Is the regulatory state doing more harm than good to the American economy?"**
- PRO: $1.9 trillion in annual compliance costs, regulatory capture by incumbents, barriers to entry for small business, occupational licensing restricts 30% of workforce
- CON: Environmental protection, consumer safety, financial stability (2008 was deregulation failure), child labor/workplace safety history

**"Should most federal programs be devolved to state governments?"**
- PRO: Laboratories of democracy, closer to constituents, competition between states, 10th Amendment, one-size-fits-all fails, states better at innovation
- CON: Race to the bottom, interstate coordination problems, small states can't handle large programs, civil rights history

## Democracy & Governance

**⭐ "Is democracy the best system of governance, or are there fundamental flaws that make it unsustainable?"**
- PRO democracy: Churchill's "worst except all others," peaceful power transitions, protects minority rights (with constitutional framework), accountability, legitimacy
- CON/critique: Rational ignorance (Caplan's "Myth of the Rational Voter"), short-term bias, mob rule/tyranny of majority, Hoppe's "Democracy: The God That Failed," public choice theory, regulatory capture, uninformed voters making complex policy decisions

**"Is a constitutional republic with strict limits on government power preferable to a majoritarian democracy?"**
- PRO republic: Bill of Rights protects individual rights from majority, prevents tyranny of majority, founders' intent, property rights protection
- CON: Minority rule through structural advantages (Senate, Electoral College), counter-majoritarian difficulty, democratic legitimacy requires majority rule

**"Has the expansion of voting and democratic participation improved or degraded the quality of governance?"**
- PRO expansion: More legitimate, diverse perspectives improve policy, prevents tyranny by narrow interests
- CON: Caplan's rational irrationality, Brennan's "Against Democracy" (epistocracy argument), low-information voting, special interest capture of democratic process

## Individual Liberty & Rights

**"Should individuals have the right to engage in any voluntary transaction between consenting adults, even if society disapproves?"**
- PRO: Mill's harm principle, bodily autonomy, voluntary exchange is inherently just, prohibition creates black markets and violence, war on drugs failure
- CON: Externalities, power imbalances in "voluntary" transactions, community standards, some paternalism protects vulnerable

**"Is taxation theft, or a legitimate obligation of citizenship?"**
- PRO "taxation is theft": Property rights are natural rights, coercion is coercion regardless of who does it, Lysander Spooner, no explicit consent, government is not a voluntary association
- CON: Social contract, public goods can't be provided privately, benefits of civilization, consent through democratic process, free rider problem

**"Should the government have any role in regulating what individuals put in their own bodies?"**
- PRO regulation: Addiction externalities, public health costs, children's exposure, opioid crisis shows industry can't self-regulate
- CON: Bodily autonomy is the most fundamental right, drug war has been catastrophic failure, Portugal decriminalization success, prohibition creates crime

## Monetary Policy & Theory

**"Has the abandonment of the gold standard in 1971 been a net positive or negative for the American economy?"**
- PRO (leaving gold was good): Enabled flexible monetary policy, prevented deflation spirals, allowed economic growth not limited by gold supply, most prosperous era in history followed
- CON (leaving gold was bad): Dollar lost 87%+ of purchasing power since 1971, enabled endless deficit spending, wage stagnation began in 1971, housing/education/healthcare cost explosion, WTFhappenedin1971.com

**"Is fractional reserve banking fundamentally fraudulent or a legitimate financial innovation?"**
- PRO (it's fraud): Creating money from nothing, bank runs prove instability, Murray Rothbard's critique, moral hazard, privatized gains/socialized losses
- CON (it's innovation): Credit creation enables economic growth, deposit insurance solved bank runs, maturity transformation serves real function, full reserve banking would freeze lending

**⭐ "Should the United States adopt a rules-based monetary policy (like the Taylor Rule or fixed money supply growth) instead of discretionary Federal Reserve management?"**
- PRO rules: Removes political manipulation, predictability helps planning, Friedman's k-percent rule, Taylor Rule would have prevented housing bubble, reduces moral hazard
- CON discretion: Crises require flexibility, no rule can anticipate all scenarios, 2008/COVID needed extraordinary response, rules become obsolete
